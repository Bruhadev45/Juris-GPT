Feature 01

Dashboard

Your command center — see everything at a glance

What You See

Sidebar → Dashboard

The Dashboard is the first screen you land on after logging in. It gives you an overview of your legal workspace with live data from the backend.

  • Document Status Card — Shows the latest document being reviewed, its AI analysis progress (%), and current review stage
  • AI Summary & Risk — Highlights the highest-risk clause found by AI, with risk zone score and clause type
  • AI Recommendation — Actionable suggestion from the AI with a "See Suggested Rewrite" button
  • Documents List — Quick access to your recent documents with view and download buttons
  • Key Metrics — Four stat cards: Legal Cases indexed, Companies Act sections, Indian Laws, and Total Sections
  • Companies Act Sections — Shows top 5 relevant sections from the Companies Act 2013
  • Relevant Cases — Table of relevant case law with case name, court, year, principle, and relevance score

How to Use It

1

Check Your Document Status

Look at the top-left card to see which document is currently being analyzed and its progress percentage.

2

Review AI Insights

The AI Summary card shows risk zones. Click "See Suggested Rewrite" on the Recommendation card to see AI-proposed changes.

3

Quick Actions

Use the top-right buttons: "Legal Chat" opens the AI assistant, "New Agreement" starts a Founder Agreement.

4

Browse Legal Data

Scroll down to see Companies Act sections and relevant cases. Click any case row for details.

Feature 02

Cases

Manage and search your legal case database

What You See

Sidebar → Cases

A searchable list of legal cases with details including court, citation, legal principle, and summary.

How to Use It

1

Search Existing Cases

Type in the search bar at the top. It searches across case names, principles, and summaries with a 300ms debounce.

2

Add a New Case

Click "New Case" in the header. Fill in: Case Name (required), Citation (e.g., "(1973) 4 SCC 225"), Court (required), Legal Principle, Summary, and Relevance. Click "Add Case".

3

View Case Details

Click any case card to navigate to its detail page. Each card shows the case name, summary (first 2 lines), citation, court, and relevant badges.

Feature 03

Legal Search

Search across 16M+ Indian judgments, statutes, and regulations

What You See

Sidebar → Legal Search

A powerful search engine that queries across three Indian legal databases simultaneously: Case Law, Companies Act 2013, and various Statutes (IPC, CPC, CrPC, etc.).

How to Use It

1

Enter Your Search Query

Type at least 2 characters in the search bar. Search starts automatically after 400ms. Try suggested queries like "Contract breach" or "Specific performance".

2

Filter by Type

Click one of the four filter buttons: All Case Law Statutes Companies Act. Each filter narrows results to that category only.

3

Select a Statute (Optional)

When "All" or "Statutes" filter is active, a dropdown appears. Select a specific law (IPC, CPC, CrPC, etc.) to browse its sections.

4

Browse Results

Results are color-coded: Case Law (blue), Companies Act (green), Statute (purple). Each result shows the title, court/section, and summary.

5

Navigate Pages

Use the Previous/Next buttons at the top-right of results. 10 results per page.

Tip: Click any suggested search term (e.g., "Director duties", "Arbitration clause") on the empty state to instantly search for it.
Feature 04

Smart Review

Upload documents and get AI-powered legal analysis

What You See

Sidebar → Smart Review

A two-panel interface: the left panel for uploading documents and managing your document list, the right panel for viewing detailed AI analysis results.

How to Use It

1

Upload a Document

Drag and drop a file onto the upload zone in the left panel, or click it to browse. Accepted formats: PDF, DOC, DOCX. Max size: 10MB.

2

Trigger AI Analysis

After upload, the document appears in the list with status Pending. Click the "Analyze" button on the document card to start AI analysis.

3

View Analysis Results

Once analysis completes (status changes to Completed), click the document to see results in the right panel.

Understanding the Analysis

The right panel shows four sections when a document is analyzed:

SectionWhat It Shows
SummaryAI-generated plain-English summary of the document
Risk ScoreOverall risk (0-100) with color coding: green (≤30), yellow (31-60), red (>60)
Clause AnalysisGrid showing which standard clauses are present or missing (e.g., Indemnity, Termination, IP)
AI SuggestionsNumbered list of recommended improvements to the document
Feature 05

Chat (AI Legal Assistant)

Ask legal questions and get AI-powered answers

What You See

Sidebar → Chat

A conversational AI interface similar to ChatGPT, specialized for Indian legal queries. The AI uses your legal database (case law, statutes) to provide informed answers.

How to Use It

1

Start with a Quick Action or Free Text

On the empty screen, you see three action cards: Research Case Law, Draft Document, and Analyze Document. Click one, or type your question directly in the input box.

2

Send Your Message

Type your legal question and press Enter to send. Use Shift + Enter for a new line without sending.

3

Read AI Responses

The AI responds with formatted Markdown including headings, bullet points, bold text, and legal citations. Your messages appear on the right (blue), AI responses on the left.

4

Continue the Conversation

Ask follow-up questions. The AI maintains context throughout the conversation.

Example questions: "What are the legal requirements for terminating an employee in India?", "Explain Section 138 of the Negotiable Instruments Act", "Draft a notice for breach of contract"
Feature 06

RTI Assistant

Generate Right to Information applications in 4 easy steps

What You See

Sidebar → RTI Assistant

A step-by-step wizard that guides you through creating a formal RTI (Right to Information Act, 2005) application addressed to any Indian government department.

How to Use It

1

Step 1: Select Department

Choose from 16 government departments displayed as cards (e.g., Finance, Health, Education, Railways, Home Affairs, Law & Justice). Click a card to select it, then click "Next".

2

Step 2: Provide Details

Enter the Subject of your RTI (required), describe the Information Requested (required), and optionally state the Purpose/Reason. Click "Next".

3

Step 3: Applicant Information

Fill in your Full Name (required), Address (required), Phone (optional), Email (optional). The PIO address is auto-filled based on the department you selected but can be edited.

4

Step 4: Review & Generate

Review all details in a summary view. Click "Generate RTI Application". The AI generates a formally worded RTI application following the proper legal format.

5

Download the Application

After generation, you'll see a preview of the application text. Click "Download Application" to save it as a .txt file. Click "Create New Application" to start over.

Tip: The generated application includes all required sections: date, addressee, subject, information sought, fee details, applicant info, and a formal declaration — ready to submit.
Feature 07

Compliance View

Track all Indian regulatory deadlines — GST, ROC, TDS, PF/ESI, Income Tax, Board Meetings

What You See

Sidebar → Compliance View

A dashboard showing all upcoming Indian compliance deadlines with urgency levels, categories, and status tracking.

How to Use It

1

Check Summary Cards

At the top, three cards show: Total Deadlines (with % on track), Overdue (red, need immediate attention), and Due This Week (yellow).

2

Filter by Category

Click category tabs to filter: All, GST (GSTR-1, GSTR-3B, GSTR-9), ROC (AOC-4, MGT-7, DIR-3 KYC), TDS (quarterly), PF/ESI (monthly), Income Tax (advance tax, ITR), Board Meetings. Each tab shows its count.

3

Read Deadline Cards

Each card has a colored left border indicating urgency: Critical red, High orange, Medium yellow, Low green. You'll see the due date, days remaining, recurrence info, and penalty warning.

Important: Deadlines marked Overdue require immediate attention. These have negative days remaining and may carry penalties.
Feature 08

Compliance Calendar

Visual month-view calendar of all compliance deadlines

What You See

Sidebar → Compliance Calendar

A full month grid calendar with colored dots showing deadline urgency on each day. Same data as Compliance View but in a visual calendar format.

How to Use It

1

Navigate Months

Use the ← and → arrows next to the month name to go to previous/next months. Click "Today" to jump back to the current month.

2

Read the Color Dots

Each day cell shows small dots. Colors match urgency: • Critical, • High, • Medium, • Low. A "+N" appears if there are more than 4 deadlines on a day.

3

Click a Day

Click any day with deadlines to open a detail panel below the calendar. It lists all deadlines for that day with full details: title, description, urgency, status, category, and penalty info.

4

Check "Upcoming This Week"

Below the calendar, the "Upcoming This Week" panel shows all deadlines due in the next 7 days, sorted by date, with relative time indicators (Today, Tomorrow, 3d, etc.).

Feature 10

Agreements (Founder Agreement)

Create a comprehensive Founder Agreement with a 3-step wizard

What You See

Sidebar → Legal Forms → Founder Agreement "Create" button
Or: Dashboard → "New Agreement" button

A 3-step form wizard that collects company details, founder information, and legal preferences to generate a complete Founder Agreement document.

How to Use It

1

Step 1: Company Information

Enter: Company Name (required), Description, State (required), and Authorized Capital in ₹ (required). Click "Next: Add Founders".

2

Step 2: Founder Details

Add 2–4 founders. For each founder, enter: Name, Email, Role, Equity %, Vesting Period (months, default 48), and Cliff Period (months, default 12). Total equity must equal exactly 100%. Click "Add Founder" to add more (max 4).

3

Step 3: Legal Preferences

Choose: Non-Compete clause (checkbox, with period if enabled), Dispute Resolution method (Arbitration / Court / Mediation), and Additional Terms (optional free text). Click "Create Agreement".

4

View Your Agreement

After creation, you are redirected to the agreement detail page at /agreements/[id] where you can view the generated document, download it, and track its review status.

Validation: All founder equity percentages must add up to exactly 100%. The form will not proceed if this requirement is not met.
Feature 11

Contract Analyzer

Deep clause-by-clause analysis with risk scoring

What You See

Sidebar → Contract Analyzer

An advanced document analysis tool that extracts and evaluates 8 types of legal clauses from uploaded contracts, providing risk scores and actionable suggestions.

How to Use It

1

Upload a Contract

Drag and drop a file onto the upload area, or click to browse. Accepted: PDF, DOC, DOCX.

2

Wait for Analysis

The system uploads the file, then automatically starts analysis. You'll see progress bars for both uploading and analyzing stages.

3

Review Results

The analysis screen shows five sections (see table below). Click "Analyze Another" in the header to start over with a new contract.

Understanding the Analysis

SectionDetails
Risk ScoreScore out of 10 with color circle: green (<4 = Low), yellow (4-6 = Medium), red (>6 = High)
SummaryAI-generated overview of the contract's purpose and key terms
Clause Table8 clauses analyzed: Indemnity, Termination, Non-Compete, IP, Force Majeure, Confidentiality, Limitation of Liability, Governing Law. Each shows: Present/Missing/Risky status, risk level
Risk BreakdownHorizontal bar chart showing risk level per clause with color-coded bars
AI SuggestionsNumbered list of specific improvements (e.g., "Add a mutual indemnification clause")
Tip: Contract Analyzer provides deeper analysis than Smart Review. Use Smart Review for quick checks and Contract Analyzer for thorough clause-by-clause evaluation.

Feature 13

Document Vault

Securely store, organize, and retrieve all your legal documents

What You See

Sidebar → Document Vault

A file management system with category organization, tagging, and grid/list views. Think of it as a dedicated "Google Drive" for your legal documents.

How to Use It

1

Upload a Document

Click "Upload Document" in the header. In the dialog: select a file, choose a Category (Agreements, Contracts, Compliance, Tax, Employment, Corporate, Court Documents), add an optional description, and enter comma-separated tags.

2

Filter by Category

Click the category tabs (All, Agreements, Contracts, etc.) to filter documents. Only documents in that category will be shown.

3

Switch View

Toggle between Grid View (cards in 3 columns) and List View (compact rows) using the toggle buttons next to the category tabs.

4

Download or Delete

Each document card has a "Download" button and a red trash icon to delete. Deleting asks for confirmation first.
